Uber suffered a data breach in 2014 that affected 50,000 Uber drivers across the U.S., the ride-sharing startup disclosed in a statement on Friday.The company determined on September 17, 2014 that a third party could have accessed one of its databases.After Uber “changed the access protocols for the database” and looked into the situation, it learned through an investigation that someone apparently accessed one of its databases on May 13, 2014, wrote Katherine M Tassi, Uber’s managing counsel, privacy.
